Elana’s Story

Elana Laila Koenig stands four-foot-eight with a voice that challenges her small frame. She’s twelve years old, but when she speaks, everyone listens. Wrinkled adults in expensive suits lean in. Rambunctious children quiet down. When she sings, her voice carries from the bottom of her soul, eager to tell the story of a survivor. At the age of 8, Elana Laila Koenig was diagnosed with Ewing’s Sarcoma, a rare bone cancer. At the age of 12, she started a foundation to help alleviate the burdens of childhood cancer. In her petite frame, she embodies something bigger than all of us: hope.

As Seen On...

﻿Elana Laila Koenig is interested in more than just assisting families. She’s interested in raising awareness and building communities of healing. To that extent, we’ve been interviewed on CBS2 News, featured on SomeGoodNews Instagram, spoken at the World Net Summit, met prominent NYC politicians Senator Liz Kreuger and Congresswoman Carolyn Maloney, and more.

Our Mission

﻿We’ve built a child-led community of healing that individualizes support to families fighting childhood cancer. We’re there from diagnosis to beyond remission, because the financial and emotional burden of illness lingers even if cancer itself does not. We help pay for uninsured medical expenses, donate toys, host events, raise awareness, politically advocate, and serve as a constant source of support for the families we adopt. Because we care.
